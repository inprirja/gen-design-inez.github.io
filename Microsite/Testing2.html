Struggles

The process of conducting it was quite a handful to do, since in the end it was Brice's program that we decided to the perform user-testing. His one requires a screen-capture app so that we can capture the gesture of the audience as well as the 'drawing' that they did based on their hand gesture. Since I live with 4 of my roomates, it seems logical that it was me that did the user testing. So I did. The first thing that I searched whose room I want to conduct the user testing in. I decided to use Sabrina's! Since her is the most well-lit compared to the rest of me and my roomates room.
But besides that, I also tried and borrowed Sabrina's laptop. Since I'm using Windows and she uses mac, I thought it would be easier to use a laptop where there is already a pre-built screencapture app. Sabrina also helped me a lot in terms of setting up. I asked all of my roomate to help me with the user-testing, and they did!

Insight

As I mentioned before, the 3 first user tester I used Sabrina's mac. Looking at the Q & A asnwer, 3 of them synonymously agree that the program is laggy. But there is a question whether or not is the lag caused by the computer or the program itself? So I decided to try and use my own laptop instead. It took a while to download and search for the screen capture app for Windows. But when I tried the program in my computer, it doesn’t lag as much. Proven by the 2 last user tester that no longer comments on its lagginess.
Outcome-wise, after the user-testing, a lot of the people seem to understand what they are supposed to do and the movements come from their hand gestures. Some note that the choosing of color is appropriate and nice. However, only 1 out of the 5 users that we test seem to see the connection between the project’s outcome and our themes which briefly touch on emotion. 2 out of the 5 users feels that the project makes them more aware of their own gestures and the speed of their hands. However, a lot of them seem to think that it is a fascinating project, and the idea of engagement and drawing with their body is interesting. One thing that I observe during the user testing – not sure how significant this is – is that depending on the computer, the program might be lagging. Therefore, in the future if there is the opportunity, it would be better to present this program using better equipment and perhaps even use a projector. It would provide better visualisation and a lot more fun, especially considering the length of width of the space.

Personal Reflection

I would say that the outcome for the p5js program is as expected! I’ve seen Brice code and watching him throughout the process, I can see how the outcome might be. Whereas for the video and the documentation of the user testing, kind of but I still feel like it doesn’t feel as nice as it can be. I am the one who documents the user testing, as all 5 of them combinations of my housemates and friends. But during the process of capturing the interactions, I struggle on finding the best ways to show what the program’s potential as well as capability, and also the place which I conduct the user testing is not great either. I feel like if given the chance, it would be better to perform the user testing with better equipment and in a much better place than now. As for the outcome and observations, I find that some of the insight from my friends are quite interesting and it is fun to be able to see them interact with the program. From the project overall, I learn that to be able to code takes time, but I do want to learn more in the future. Whereas team-wise, I learn to be more open and communicate more to my teammates through my ideas and to initiate certain things or to propose the next step of the process. All in all, this has been a fun experience.
